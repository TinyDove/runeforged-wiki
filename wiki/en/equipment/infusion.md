# Infusion

Magic, Rare, Legendary, Mythic, and Cursed equipment can gain Infusion Value. At `100%`, the item gains a separate Infusion Affix that does not consume a regular-affix slot.

## Gaining Infusion Value

When a player kills a monster, the killing weapon and eligible worn armor gain Infusion Value.

`Base Value = (1 + Monster Level × 0.02) × (1 + Elite Affix count)`

Each receiving item then rolls its own `0.5–1.5` multiplier. The weapon receives the full Base Value; all eligible worn armor pieces split the Base Value evenly before their individual random multipliers.

| Monster source | Multiplier |
| --- | ---: |
| Normal spawn | 100% |
| Spawner | 25% |
| Split medium Slime or duplicated Shulker | 25% |
| Split small Slime, Vex, Endermite, or Silverfish | 5% |
| Summoning Aura reinforcement | 0% |

## Infusion Capacity

| Material | Base weapon capacity |
| --- | ---: |
| Wood, Stone, Gold | 100 |
| Copper, Iron | 200 |
| Diamond, Netherite | 400 |
| Other eligible weapons | 200 |

- Armor capacity is `50%` of the matching material's base capacity.
- A Normal Cursed item's first Infusion requires `50%` of base weapon capacity.
- After an item already has an Infusion Affix, its next cycle requires `50%` capacity.
- Nature Rune-Cursed Weapons use full weapon capacity and support repeated cycles.

For example, an Iron weapon requires `200`, Iron armor `100`, and a Normal Cursed Iron weapon `100`. Iron armor preparing a reroll after gaining an Infusion Affix requires `50`.

## At 100%

- No Infusion Affix: the first affix is created immediately and progress returns to `0%`.
- Existing Infusion Affix: progress remains at `100%`, ready for reinfusion.
- Using an Infusion Gem at `100%` replaces the affix and clears progress.
- Normal, Sealed, and Alien-Cursed equipment ends after its first Infusion Affix and cannot accumulate or reroll again.
- Nature Rune-Cursed Weapons can keep accumulating, reroll at `100%`, or use an Infusion Gem directly.

Generated Cursed equipment may begin with `0–75%` Infusion Value.

## Infusion Gems

| Currency | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Infusion Gem | Adds `25–100` Infusion Value while incomplete; rerolls at `100%`; directly infuses a Nature Rune-Cursed Weapon and rolls the chosen affix's value three times, keeping the best |
| Celestial Infusion Gem | Creates an Infusion Affix regardless of progress; chooses the affix once and rolls its value three times, keeping the best |

A Celestial Infusion Gem cannot unlock a Normal Cursed item whose one infusion is already complete.

## Weapon Infusion Affixes

Weapon Infusion Affixes are T2 and filtered by weapon type, existing affixes, and conflicts.

| Group | Candidates |
| --- | --- |
| Damage | Physical Damage; eligible Fire, Frost, Lightning, or Holy Damage |
| General attack | Attack Speed, Critical Strike Chance, Elemental Resistance Penetration, Armor Penetration |
| Ranged | Ammo Conservation, Bow Draw Speed, Crossbow Charge Speed, Multishot, Long-Range Damage |
| Slayer | Undead Slayer, Human Slayer, Giant Slayer |
| Blessing | Blessing of the Sun, Blessing of Shadows, Blessing of Storms, Blessing of Otherworlds |

If the weapon already has elemental flat or compound damage, elemental infusion selects only the element with the highest existing flat total. With no elemental damage, all four elements are candidates. Greatswords may receive Attack Speed through Infusion.

Slayer affixes occupy one candidate group, and Blessings occupy one group. Entering a group then chooses among its eligible members, so three Slayers or four Blessings do not each receive a full top-level weight.

## Armor Infusion Affixes

Armor Infusion Affixes are T3. Candidates are Max Health, Armor, Armor Toughness, Melee/Projectile/Explosion/Fire Damage Reduction, the four Shelters, and Knockback Resistance. Slot-specific helmet, chestplate, leggings, and boots affixes cannot be generated by Infusion.
